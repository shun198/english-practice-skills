### Usable Themes

- English as a practical communication protocol for engineers.
- Low-latency English for meetings, Slack, code reviews, and incidents.
- Basic grammar as a system: sentence patterns, articles, prepositions,
  questions, negation, tense, and conjunctions.
- Core verbs and phrasal verbs: run, make, get, take, put, go, look, keep,
  follow up, go through, look into, take ownership, and get back to.
- Engineering writing contexts: Pull Requests, Issues, comments, commit
  messages, release notes, bug reports, progress updates, and access requests.
- Meeting and chat patterns: scheduling, rescheduling, ETA checks, reminders,
  confirmation, agreement, disagreement, appreciation, and handoff.
- Technical vocabulary and jargon: LGTM, FYI, TBH, ETA, EOD, workaround,
  technical debt, dogfooding, bikeshedding, rubber duck debugging, rollback,
  sandbox, production, refactor, deprecate, and obsolete.
- Direct-translation repair for Japanese engineering phrases, including
  `memory is heavy/eats memory`, `version up`, `cost down`, `claim`, `note PC`,
  and overly direct requests.
